From Ideas to Action: A Guide for Young Entrepreneurs


View Website & Workbook
Co-Author and Co-Designer (book)
Designer and Developer (website)
Editing (video)

Context // One of the best ways to empower youth is by giving them the ability to participate in the economic system. Business courses in Ghana, Rwanda, Tanzania, and Uganda (if there are any) are not typically taught with the context of a young entrepreneur in mind. Babson Entrepreneurial Leadership Academies (BELA) gather top students from around the region in an intensive, 6-day academy that culminates in each student delivering a three minute rocket pitch about a business idea they could start today.

Problem // How might we provide the students with a way to capture and track their progress on their entrepreneurial journey? How might we lower the perceived barriers (like not having enough money or support or education) to taking action on an idea? How might we encourage students to continue to work on their ideas even after their week at BELA? How might we support BELA teachers who are often teaching entrepreneurship in this context for the first time?

Solution // We created a workbook titled "From Ideas to Action" that secondary school students who attend BELA can individually work-through to develop product or service ideas that can be implemented TODAY. In designing it we placed an emphasis on using relatable examples to a young entrepreneur. The book is approximately 250 pages. The content is divided into three sections (Thinking, Planning, and Doing) which are linked together though reflection and iteration. The layout and design of the workbook reinforces this through built-in exercises, margins for doodling, and opportunities for guided reflection. The book is accompanied by a CD with extended video lessons for each chapter.

Testing and Iteration // I taught at BELA using the "From Ideas to Action" workbook in Ghana, Rwanda, and Uganda. As a BELA teacher, I worked with a small group of approximately 10 students in each country and was able to see first hand what was working and what was not. The book is under constant revision as we use it ourselves and get feedback from those using it.
